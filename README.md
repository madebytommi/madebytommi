# Hi, I’m Tommi.

### I build humane, weird, independent technology for people who do not fit comfortably inside mainstream systems.

I’m a longtime web tinkerer, IT support professional, and human-centered systems builder.

My work lives where practical problem-solving, accessibility, privacy, workflow design, independent technology, and old-internet culture overlap.

I care about technology that gives people more agency instead of demanding more attention, data, energy, or conformity from them.

## What I build

### Human-centered tools

Technology designed around real human limitations, overlooked needs, and imperfect circumstances.

* **[Unseen and Strong](https://github.com/madebytommi/unseen-and-strong)**
  An offline-first Android app supporting people living with chronic and invisible illness through journaling, routines, accommodation tracking, advocacy tools, and low-pressure planning.

* **[PainCast](https://github.com/madebytommi/paincast)**
  A weather-based planning tool that translates temperature, pressure, and humidity into an explainable chronic-pain forecast.

### Independent and privacy-minded technology

Experiments involving local control, open tools, private communication, model choice, and alternatives to locked-down platforms.

* **[MutinyChat](https://github.com/madebytommi/mutinychat)**
  A prototype encrypted desktop messenger built with Tauri, Rust, Svelte, Python, and Tor hidden services.

* **[OpenKitten](https://github.com/madebytommi/openkitten)**
  A Discord-first AI coding bot with model switching, OpenCode integration, tests, linting, and controlled configuration.

* **[Mutiny Bot](https://github.com/madebytommi/mutiny-bot)**
  A local-first AI assistant experiment involving automation, scheduled tasks, RSS monitoring, and persistent storage.

### Old-internet experiments

The web used to feel personal, strange, expressive, and alive. I still think it should.

* **[Cyber Portfolio ’99](https://github.com/madebytommi/x-portfolio)**
* **[Dial-Up Fun](https://github.com/madebytommi/dial-up-fun)**
* **[Y2K Oracle](https://github.com/madebytommi/Y2K-Oracle)**

## Systems, workflows, and the people inside them

I’m interested in the invisible systems that shape people’s experiences with technology: how they ask for help, how information moves, how decisions are made, and what happens when a process fails.

My background in technical support has shown me that many “user problems” are really system problems—unclear instructions, unnecessary steps, inaccessible interfaces, missing context, or workflows designed around the organization instead of the person using them.

I enjoy taking complicated processes and making them easier to understand, maintain, and navigate.

Some of the areas I’m currently exploring include:

* human-centered workflow and service design
* automation that reduces repetitive work without removing human judgment
* clearer support, intake, and request experiences
* accessible documentation and interfaces
* thoughtful permissions and responsible data handling
* systems that explain what they are doing instead of making users guess

I’m especially interested in the space between technical configuration and human experience:

> How do we make complicated systems clearer, fairer, and less exhausting for the people who have to use them?

## Principles behind my work

* Build for people, not engagement metrics.
* Explain what the system is doing.
* Preserve user agency.
* Reduce shame, friction, and unnecessary complexity.
* Make accessibility part of the design, not an afterthought.
* Keep technology useful, personal, and a little strange.
* Do not make people fight the system harder than necessary.
